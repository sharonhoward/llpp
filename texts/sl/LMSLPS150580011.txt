To the Right Honourable William Benn Esquire Lord Mayor of the City of London and the rest of the Worshipfull his Majesties Justices of the Peace of the said City at the General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition of Thomas Branstone of the Parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London Cooper

Sheweth That by Warrant under the Hand and Seal of John Blackford Esquire Alderman of the Ward of Cripplegate without Dated the 20th. day of April last your Petitioner was Appointed and Confirmed one of the Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Giles without Cripplegate London aforesaid for this present year

That your Petitioners business requiring much of his Personal Application thereto and your petitioner also being but in an Indifferent State of health is unable to perform the said Office but is William to Fine for the same According to the Custom of the said parish and having Applied for that purpose the Parishoners have agreed to Accept of your Petitioners Fine and that your Petitioner may be Discharged from the said Office If this Honourable Court Shall think fit

Your Petitioner therefore most Humbly prays That he may be Discharged from the said Office by Order of this Court

and your petitioner as in Duty bound shall ever pray Etc

Thos. Branston

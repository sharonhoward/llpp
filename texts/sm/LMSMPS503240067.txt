the Right Worshipfull His Majestys Justices of the peace for the County of Middlesex,

The humble Appeal of the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Peter le Poor London

Sheweth. That by an Order dated the 23d. day of June Last under the hands and Seals of John Milner and John Mercer Esqrs. two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex (one being of the Quorum) One Mary Randall Widow of Thomas Randall deced was passed from the parish of St. Giles in the Fields in the said County to the said parish of St. Peter le Poor in London as the place of her last Legall Setlement

That the said Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Peter le Poor Conceive themselves aggrieved by the said Order

Wm: Iliffe And therefore pray this Honoble Court to appoint a day to hear the said Appeal against the Said Order And your Petrs. Shall ever pray etc,

Middx At the Generall Quarter sessions of the Peace of our Sovereign Lord the King holden for the County of Middlesex at Hiks hall in St. John Street in the County aforesaid on Monday the fourth day of July in the Eleventh year of the reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Second King of Great Britain France and Ireland Defender of the Faith

It is Ordered by this Court that the Church wardens and Overseers of the Poor of the parish of St. Giles int the Fields in this County have notice of this Appeal, and that they and all persons concerned Do attend this Court on Friday next at nine of the Clock in the forenoon to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of this Court touching the said Appeal.

By the Court

Walter

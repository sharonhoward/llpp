To the Worshipful his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex in their present General Quarter Session of the Peace Assembled

The Humble Petition of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Clement Danes in the County of Middlesex and Liberty of Westminster

Sheweth That by an Order or Pass Warrant under the hands and Seals of Edward Pepys and Samuel Foyster Esquires Two of his Majestys Justices of the Peace in and for the County of Middlesex (One whereof being of the Quorum) bearing date the 14th.. day of February 1797 Robert Jones and Mary Ann his wife and their Two Children namely Maria aged Five years and an half and a Male Child born on the Second day of February 1797 not then Captired were removed and conveyed from and out of the Parish of Saint Pancras in the County of Middlesex to the said parish of Saint Clement Danes as the Place of their last legal Settlement

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Order of the said Two Justices appeal to your Worships there from and humbly pray that you will be pleased to hear them in the premises and Order that the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the said Parish of Saint Pancras may Appear before you on a certain day to be appointed in the next General Sessions of the peace to hear and abide the Judgment and Determination of your Worships touching the said Appeal

And Your Petitioners shall ever Pray Etc

To the Worshipfull his Majestys Justices of the Peace for the County of Middlesex at their General Session of the Peace assembled

The Humble Petition and Appeal of the Churchwardens and Overseers of the Poor of the Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the City of London

Sheweth That by Virtue of a Pass Warrant or Order of Removal under the Hands and Seals of Daniel Williams Esquire and Rice Davies Esquire two of his Majesty's Justices of the Peace acting in and for the County of Middlesex (one whereof being [---] of the Quorum) bearing date the day of July one thousand seven hundred and ninety eight Thomas Nelson was removed and conveyed [---] from and out of the Precinct of Saint Catherine in the County of Middlesex to the said Parish of Saint Botolph Aldgate in the City of London as the Place of the last legal Settlement of the said Thomas Nelson

Your Petitioners conceiving themselves aggrieved by the said Pass Warrant or Order of Removal of the said two Justices do therefore humbly Appeal to this Court against the same praying such Relief in the Premises as to this Court shall seem meet

And Your Petitioners shall ever pray Etc

[...] Solicts for the Appellants

Middlesex at the General Session of the Peace of our Lord the King holden in and for the County of Middlesex at the Session House for the said County on Monday the Tenth day of September in the Year of our Lord one thousand and seven hundred
